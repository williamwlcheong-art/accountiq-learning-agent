## 1. System Classification

**System Type:** Autonomous Task Agent (bounded agentic research loop with structured output)

**Description:**
An agentic web research loop where Claude autonomously searches the web to gather four categories of information needed for a business valuation: company profile (news, website, notable clients, significant events), sector and market context (NZ market size, growth rates, competitive dynamics), comparable M&A transactions (for the EV/EBITDA cross-check paragraph), and live WACC inputs (RBNZ 10-yr bond yield, Damodaran ERP and industry beta, Stats NZ CPI). Claude decides what to search, reads results, and continues until it has enough context to produce a structured research brief. The brief is then consumed by Python DCF computation. A separate non-agentic Claude call writes the report narrative around the computed numbers. End users are Bayleys Business advisors producing indicative valuation reports for SME clients.

**Critical Failure Modes:**
1. Fabricated WACC inputs — Claude invents a risk-free rate or ERP instead of retrieving it from RBNZ or Damodaran. Python then computes a DCF from hallucinated inputs, producing a materially wrong valuation range with no visible signal of failure.
2. Infinite loop / runaway cost — the agentic loop has no hard iteration cap and Claude keeps searching indefinitely, burning tokens and web search credits without returning a brief.
3. Structured brief returned with missing required fields — `risk_free_rate`, `erp`, `industry_beta`, or `wacc_scenarios` are None or absent, causing the Python DCF to crash or silently use defaults, corrupting the valuation output.
4. Web search tool returns an error (`max_uses_exceeded`, `unavailable`) and the loop swallows it silently — the brief is returned incomplete without the caller knowing research failed.
5. Research brief mixes up the target company with a similarly-named business — company summary contains facts from the wrong entity, embedding false context in the valuation report.

## 1b. Domain Context

> Researched by `gsd-domain-researcher`. Grounds the evaluation strategy in domain expert knowledge.

**Industry Vertical:** Finance — SME business valuation / M&A advisory (NZ/AU)

**User Population:** Bayleys Business advisors (business brokers and M&A intermediaries) preparing indicative valuation reports for SME owners considering a sale or capital raise. The report is a client-facing deliverable bearing the Bayleys brand. The immediate recipient is the SME owner; the downstream audience includes potential buyers and their advisors.

**Stakes Level:** High

**Output Consequence:** The valuation range (Low EV to High EV) derived from the DCF directly anchors the asking price or negotiating floor in a business sale transaction. A 10-15% error in WACC inputs shifts the concluded enterprise value by 20-30% in a 5-year DCF — on a $5M business, that is a $1-1.5M pricing error. Advisors sign off on reports bearing the Bayleys brand and are subject to CAANZ Code of Ethics obligations for any work product carrying their professional identity. An indicative valuation with fabricated or stale WACC inputs, or a company narrative drawn from the wrong entity, creates reputational and professional liability exposure.

Domain experts in this context are: (a) qualified business valuers holding CAANZ membership or the CA ANZ Business Valuation Specialisation, and (b) experienced business brokers who regularly anchor deal negotiations to valuation reports. The rubric ingredients below are drawn from AES-2 (Independent Business Valuation Engagements) practitioner standards, Damodaran methodology documentation, and NZ M&A advisory practice.

### Known Failure Modes in This Domain

These are failure modes observed in AI-generated financial reports and in human practitioner error patterns — not generic "hallucination."

1. **WACC recency failure.** Damodaran publishes his total beta and ERP datasets annually in January. The agent may retrieve or cite a prior-year dataset (e.g., January 2024 data) when the current-year data (January 2025) is available. RBNZ bond yields shift materially with OCR decisions — a cached or stale risk-free rate of 5.5% versus the current 4.65% produces a ~1.5 percentage point WACC error, shifting a 5-year DCF enterprise value by 10-15%. A practitioner reviewing the report will check the retrieval date; a missing date is itself a flag.

2. **Damodaran industry category mismatch.** Damodaran's total beta dataset uses US-centric SIC-style industry categories. For NZ SMEs, the agent must map the business to the closest matching Damodaran category — a frequent error is mapping a NZ software-as-a-service business to "Computers/Peripherals/Equipment" (hardware-dominated, low beta) instead of "Software (System & Application)" (higher total beta), producing an underestimated WACC and an inflated enterprise value. The category name used must be stated in the report so a reviewer can check it directly on the Damodaran website.

3. **Comparable transaction fabrication.** Public NZ M&A deal data is sparse. Disclosed EV/EBITDA multiples for private SME transactions are rarely published outside of broker press releases, which often cite estimated rather than disclosed figures. The agent, finding sparse results, may confabulate plausible-sounding deal names, acquirer/target pairs, or multiple ranges. This failure mode is hard to detect without practitioner familiarity with the NZ deal market. Evaluation must include a source-traceability check: every comparable cited must have a verifiable URL.

4. **Company identity collision.** Common NZ business names ("Propellerhead," "Silver Fern," "Kiwi Group") may return search results for multiple unrelated entities — a UK software company named Propellerhead, an NZ dairy company, and the actual valuation subject could all appear in the same search result set. The agent may mix facts from multiple entities into a single company narrative. This produces a report that describes the wrong company's revenue, clients, or history. Detection requires checking that all cited URLs point to the correct NZ entity.

5. **WACC expressed as decimal vs. percent.** Damodaran's published datasets present beta as a pure number (e.g., 1.24) and ERP as a percent (e.g., 5.94%). The RBNZ bond yield page presents rates in percent (e.g., 4.65%). The agent may retrieve the risk-free rate as 0.0465 (decimal form) instead of 4.65 (percent form), producing a WACC of ~1.6% instead of ~16% — a factor-of-10 error that Python's DCF would compute without complaint if no range validation is applied.

6. **Overconfident narrative under sparse data.** LLMs trained with preference optimization are statistically penalized for expressing uncertainty; they produce confident-sounding narratives even when web search results are thin or ambiguous (research shows hallucination rates in financial NLP contexts up to 41% in some benchmark studies). An agent that found no NZ-specific sector data may write a confident sector narrative drawn from global or US sources, without flagging the geographic mismatch. A practitioner reviewer who knows the NZ sector will immediately recognize the generic content.

### Regulatory / Compliance Context

**CAANZ / NZICA — AES-2 (Independent Business Valuation Engagements)**
AES-2 is the mandatory standard for CAANZ members in NZ providing independent business valuations. Indicative valuations — such as those produced by this system — are explicitly outside the AES-2 mandatory scope ("limited information...with no requirement to assess the reasonableness" per the standard). However, CAANZ members are still bound by the CAANZ Code of Ethics for any work product they sign off on. The distinction matters for evaluation: the AI-generated report does not need to meet AES-2's documentation and independence requirements, but it must not make claims that go beyond what an indicative valuation can defensibly support.

**International Valuation Standards (IVS) — effective 31 January 2025**
The IVS 2025 edition provides the global framework that CA ANZ references. For the DCF methodology used here, IVS 105 (Valuation Approaches and Methods) governs acceptable income approach practices. Key requirement: material assumptions (WACC, growth rate, forecast period) must be disclosed and their basis explained. The report's `wacc_assumptions` and `dcf_analysis` sections must satisfy this disclosure requirement.

**Financial Markets Conduct Act 2013 (FMCA) — financial advice boundary**
Indicative valuation reports produced for SME owners considering a sale do not themselves constitute "financial advice" under the FMCA, provided they: (1) state they are indicative only and not a recommendation to transact, (2) are prepared for a named client for a specific stated purpose, and (3) do not purport to advise on whether to buy, sell, or hold a financial product. The `disclaimer` section (D-S1) must contain language reflecting all three conditions. A report that says "we recommend a sale price of $X" rather than "the DCF analysis indicates an enterprise value range of $X to $Y" crosses into regulated financial advice territory.

**No additional data licences required.** RBNZ, Stats NZ, and Damodaran publish their datasets publicly on the web. The `web_search` tool retrieves current data at generation time with no API key or licence agreement needed.

## 2. Framework Decision

**Selected Framework:** Anthropic Python SDK — direct API, no agent framework

**Version:** `anthropic>=0.40.0` (already in `backend/requirements.txt`; tested against 0.49.x as of May 2026)

**Rationale:**
The use case is a bounded single-model agentic loop using one server-side tool (`web_search`) that Anthropic's API executes natively. The loop terminates when Claude stops with `end_turn` after it decides research is complete — there is no multi-agent handoff, no vector retrieval, and no stateful graph to maintain. Adding a framework (LangGraph, LlamaIndex, CrewAI) would wrap 30 lines of SDK loop logic in hundreds of lines of abstraction with no functional benefit. The existing codebase already uses the Anthropic SDK with forced tool-use (`call_claude()` in `ingestion.py`) and `asyncio.get_event_loop().run_in_executor()` for FastAPI integration — this research loop follows the identical pattern, keeping the codebase uniform. Anthropic's `web_search` tool is a server-side tool, meaning the API executes searches and injects results into the conversation automatically, which further eliminates the need for a client-side tool-dispatch layer.

**Alternatives Considered:**

| Framework | Ruled Out Because |
|-----------|------------------|
| LangGraph | Graph overhead (nodes, edges, state schema, checkpointer) is overkill for a single-model loop with one tool and a deterministic exit condition. Adds ~400 lines of scaffolding. |
| CrewAI | Designed for multi-agent role/task/crew patterns. Single-agent research with one tool doesn't fit the abstraction; would require wrapping web_search as a CrewAI tool with no benefit. |
| LlamaIndex | Data framework optimised for RAG and vector retrieval. No advantage for live web search via Anthropic's server-side tool. Would add LlamaIndex agent overhead without improving retrieval quality. |
| OpenAI Agents SDK | OpenAI-native; incompatible with Anthropic Claude models. |

**Vendor Lock-In Accepted:** Yes — the entire codebase is committed to Anthropic Claude (`claude-sonnet-4-6`). The web_search tool is Anthropic-native (server-side). Switching providers would require rewriting both the agentic loop and the report generation calls.

### Entry Point Pattern

The `web_search` tool is a **server-side tool**. Anthropic's API executes searches internally and injects `server_tool_use` and `web_search_tool_result` blocks directly into the assistant's content. The loop continues until `stop_reason == "end_turn"` — there is no client-side tool dispatch required.

```python
import asyncio
import anthropic

client = anthropic.Anthropic(api_key=os.environ["ANTHROPIC_API_KEY"])

WEB_SEARCH_TOOL = {
    "type": "web_search_20250305",   # stable version; does not require code_execution tool
    "name": "web_search",
    "max_uses": 15,                   # hard cap per research session
    "user_location": {
        "type": "approximate",
        "country": "NZ",
        "timezone": "Pacific/Auckland",
    },
}

def run_research_loop_sync(system_prompt: str, user_prompt: str) -> anthropic.types.Message:
    """
    Synchronous research loop. Call via run_in_executor from async context.
    The web_search tool is server-side: API handles search execution and
    injects results. Loop ends at end_turn — no client tool dispatch needed.
    """
    messages = [{"role": "user", "content": user_prompt}]
    iteration = 0
    max_iterations = 5   # safety: max round-trips before forcing end_turn

    while iteration < max_iterations:
        response = client.messages.create(
            model="claude-sonnet-4-6",
            max_tokens=8000,
            system=system_prompt,
            tools=[WEB_SEARCH_TOOL],
            messages=messages,
        )
        iteration += 1

        if response.stop_reason == "end_turn":
            return response

        if response.stop_reason == "max_tokens":
            raise RuntimeError("Research loop hit max_tokens — increase max_tokens or reduce scope")

        # pause_turn: API paused a long turn; pass response back as-is to continue
        if response.stop_reason == "pause_turn":
            messages.append({"role": "assistant", "content": response.content})
            continue

        # Unexpected stop_reason — surface rather than silently continue
        raise RuntimeError(f"Unexpected stop_reason: {response.stop_reason}")

    raise RuntimeError(f"Research loop exceeded {max_iterations} iterations without end_turn")


async def run_research_loop(system_prompt: str, user_prompt: str) -> anthropic.types.Message:
    """Async wrapper for FastAPI background task context."""
    loop = asyncio.get_running_loop()
    return await loop.run_in_executor(
        None, run_research_loop_sync, system_prompt, user_prompt
    )
```

### Key Abstractions

| Concept | What It Is | When You Use It |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| `web_search_20250305` | Server-side tool type string — passes the tool definition to the API. Anthropic executes the search; results appear in the response content as `web_search_tool_result` blocks. | Include in `tools=[]` on every `messages.create()` call in the research loop. Use `web_search_20260209` for dynamic filtering (requires code_execution tool also enabled). |
| `server_tool_use` content block | An assistant content block of type `server_tool_use` indicating Claude invoked web_search. Contains `id`, `name`, and `input.query`. | Read from `response.content` to log which queries Claude ran. Do not dispatch locally — the API already executed it. |
| `web_search_tool_result` content block | Contains the search results Claude received, including `encrypted_content` per result. Never parse `encrypted_content` — it is opaque and must be passed back in multi-turn payloads. | Pass the entire `response.content` back as the assistant message in a multi-turn conversation to preserve citation references. |
| `stop_reason == "end_turn"` | Claude has finished its turn naturally — it has completed all searches it wants and written its final response. | This is the loop exit condition. Extract the final text block from `response.content` to parse the research brief. |
| `stop_reason == "pause_turn"` | API paused a long-running turn (server-side); pass the response back unchanged as the next assistant message to resume. | Occurs on very long research sessions. Append `response.content` to messages and call `messages.create()` again. |
| `max_uses` | Hard cap on the number of web searches Claude can execute per `messages.create()` call. When exceeded, the tool returns `{"type": "web_search_tool_result_error", "error_code": "max_uses_exceeded"}`. | Set to 15 for research sessions. Claude sees the error and must conclude with available data. |

### Common Pitfalls

1. **Treating web_search like a client-side tool and implementing a tool dispatch loop.** The `web_search` tool is server-side — the API executes searches and injects results. You do not need to catch `stop_reason == "tool_use"`, parse tool inputs, call an external API, and return a `tool_result` message. Implementing a client-side dispatch loop will send duplicate `tool_result` messages and corrupt the conversation state.

2. **Not handling `stop_reason == "pause_turn"`.** For long research sessions (many searches, large results), the API may pause the turn mid-completion. If you treat `pause_turn` as `end_turn` and extract the response, you get an incomplete answer. Always check for `pause_turn` and resume by appending the partial response as an assistant message.

3. **Calling `asyncio.run()` inside a FastAPI async route or background task.** The FastAPI event loop is already running. `asyncio.run()` raises `RuntimeError: This event loop is already running`. Use `loop.run_in_executor(None, sync_fn)` to run the synchronous Anthropic SDK call on a thread pool — exactly the pattern in `ingestion.py`'s `call_claude()`.

4. **Parsing `encrypted_content` from search results.** Each `web_search_result` block contains an `encrypted_content` field that is opaque and version-locked. Do not attempt to decrypt or parse it. It exists only so that citations (`encrypted_index`) remain valid when the conversation is extended in a multi-turn context.

5. **Setting `max_tokens` too low for a multi-search session.** Each web search result consumes input tokens (charged as input). A 15-search session with dense results can consume 20,000–40,000 input tokens. If `max_tokens` for the output is too low (e.g., 1024), Claude will hit `stop_reason == "max_tokens"` mid-response and return a truncated brief. Set `max_tokens=8000` for the research loop output.

**Core Pattern:**

```python
# backend/research_loop.py

import os
import json
import asyncio
import logging
from typing import Optional
import anthropic
from pydantic import BaseModel, Field, ValidationError

logger = logging.getLogger(__name__)

ANTHROPIC_API_KEY = os.environ.get("ANTHROPIC_API_KEY", "")
MODEL = os.environ.get("CLAUDE_MODEL", "claude-sonnet-4-6")

WEB_SEARCH_TOOL = {
    "type": "web_search_20250305",
    "name": "web_search",
    "max_uses": 15,
    "user_location": {
        "type": "approximate",
        "country": "NZ",
        "timezone": "Pacific/Auckland",
    },
}

RESEARCH_SYSTEM_PROMPT = """You are a financial research analyst preparing inputs for a business valuation.
Your task: research the company and sector, find comparable transactions, and retrieve current WACC inputs
from authoritative NZ sources (RBNZ, Damodaran, Stats NZ).

Research in this order:
1. Company research — name, location, business model, notable clients, recent news, significant events.
2. Sector research — NZ market context, growth rates, competitors, regulatory environment.
3. Comparable M&A transactions — recent (within 3 years) NZ/ANZ sector deals with disclosed EV/EBITDA multiples.
4. WACC inputs — RBNZ 10-yr NZ govt bond yield (risk-free rate), Damodaran current-year ERP for NZ
   and total beta for the relevant industry, Stats NZ / RBNZ current NZ CPI inflation rate.

When you have sufficient data for all four categories, output a JSON object ONLY (no surrounding text)
with this exact schema:

{
  "company_summary": "string — 2-3 paragraph narrative",
  "sector_summary": "string — 2-3 paragraph narrative",
  "comparable_transactions": "string — bullet list of transactions with EV/EBITDA multiples",
  "risk_free_rate": float,            // e.g. 4.65  (percent, not decimal)
  "erp": float,                       // e.g. 5.94
  "industry_beta": float,             // e.g. 1.08  (total beta from Damodaran)
  "industry_category": "string",      // Damodaran industry category used for beta
  "inflation_rate": float,            // e.g. 2.5
  "sources": ["url1", "url2", ...]    // list of source URLs cited
}

CRITICAL: Do not return the JSON until you have retrieved risk_free_rate, erp, and industry_beta
from actual web search results. These values must come from RBNZ or Damodaran — do not estimate them."""


class ResearchBrief(BaseModel):
    company_summary: str = Field(min_length=50)
    sector_summary: str = Field(min_length=50)
    comparable_transactions: str = Field(min_length=20)
    risk_free_rate: float = Field(gt=0, lt=20)       # percent; reasonable NZ range
    erp: float = Field(gt=0, lt=20)
    industry_beta: float = Field(gt=0, lt=10)
    industry_category: str = Field(min_length=2)
    inflation_rate: float = Field(gt=-5, lt=30)
    sources: list[str] = Field(min_length=1)


def _extract_json_from_response(response: anthropic.types.Message) -> dict:
    """Extract the final text block from the response and parse as JSON."""
    text_blocks = [b for b in response.content if b.type == "text"]
    if not text_blocks:
        raise ValueError("No text block in Claude response — cannot extract research brief")
    raw = text_blocks[-1].text.strip()
    # Claude may wrap JSON in a code fence despite instructions
    if raw.startswith("```"):
        raw = raw.split("```")[1]
        if raw.startswith("json"):
            raw = raw[4:]
        raw = raw.strip()
    return json.loads(raw)


def run_research_loop_sync(
    company_name: str,
    company_location: str,
    industry_sector: str,
    max_retries: int = 2,
) -> ResearchBrief:
    """
    Synchronous Anthropic SDK call. Run via run_in_executor — never call directly
    from an async context.
    """
    client = anthropic.Anthropic(api_key=ANTHROPIC_API_KEY)

    user_prompt = (
        f"Research this business for a valuation report:\n"
        f"Company: {company_name}\n"
        f"Location: {company_location}\n"
        f"Sector: {industry_sector}\n\n"
        f"Use web_search to retrieve current data. Return the structured JSON brief when complete."
    )

    messages = [{"role": "user", "content": user_prompt}]
    iteration = 0
    max_iterations = 5

    while iteration < max_iterations:
        response = client.messages.create(
            model=MODEL,
            max_tokens=8000,
            system=RESEARCH_SYSTEM_PROMPT,
            tools=[WEB_SEARCH_TOOL],
            messages=messages,
        )
        iteration += 1

        # Log search activity for cost tracking
        search_count = getattr(response.usage, "server_tool_use", {})
        logger.info(
            "Research loop iter=%d stop_reason=%s searches=%s input_tokens=%d output_tokens=%d",
            iteration,
            response.stop_reason,
            search_count,
            response.usage.input_tokens,
            response.usage.output_tokens,
        )

        if response.stop_reason == "end_turn":
            break

        if response.stop_reason == "max_tokens":
            raise RuntimeError(
                f"Research loop hit max_tokens at iteration {iteration}. "
                "Increase max_tokens or reduce MAX_SEARCHES."
            )

        if response.stop_reason == "pause_turn":
            # API paused long turn — append partial response and resume
            messages.append({"role": "assistant", "content": response.content})
            messages.append({
                "role": "user",
                "content": "Please continue and return the JSON brief when ready."
            })
            continue

        raise RuntimeError(f"Unexpected stop_reason: {response.stop_reason!r}")
    else:
        raise RuntimeError(f"Research loop exceeded {max_iterations} iterations without end_turn")

    # Parse and validate structured brief
    raw_dict = _extract_json_from_response(response)
    try:
        brief = ResearchBrief(**raw_dict)
    except ValidationError as exc:
        raise ValueError(f"Research brief failed Pydantic validation: {exc}") from exc

    return brief


async def run_valuation_research(
    company_name: str,
    company_location: str,
    industry_sector: str,
) -> ResearchBrief:
    """
    Async entry point for FastAPI background tasks.
    Wraps synchronous SDK call in thread pool executor.
    """
    loop = asyncio.get_running_loop()
    return await loop.run_in_executor(
        None,
        run_research_loop_sync,
        company_name,
        company_location,
        industry_sector,
    )
```

**Tool Use:**

The `web_search` tool is server-side. Pass it in `tools=[]` — no client-side dispatch required. Key configuration decisions:

- `type: "web_search_20250305"` — stable version; does not require the `code_execution` tool. The newer `web_search_20260209` (with dynamic filtering) requires `code_execution` to also be enabled and is not needed here.
- `max_uses: 15` — caps the number of searches per session. Damodaran + RBNZ + Stats NZ + company + sector + comps typically requires 8–12 searches. 15 gives headroom without open-ended cost exposure.
- `user_location: {country: "NZ"}` — biases search results toward NZ sources (RBNZ, Stats NZ, NZX, NZ press).
- No `allowed_domains` filter — Damodaran (damodaran.com), RBNZ (rbnz.govt.nz), Stats NZ (stats.govt.nz), and company websites vary per engagement. Restricting domains would break the research for many companies.

**State Management:**

The research loop is stateless between FastAPI requests. All conversational state (the growing `messages` list) is held in local memory within `run_research_loop_sync()` for the duration of one call. There is no persistent state to manage:

- The `messages` list grows as `pause_turn` resumes are appended. Maximum practical size: ~60K tokens across 5 iterations.
- The validated `ResearchBrief` Pydantic object is the sole output. It is serialised to a Python dict and stored in the existing `report_intake.answers` JSON field alongside the intake questionnaire answers (D-I3 decision).
- WACC inputs from the brief are immediately consumed by `compute_wacc_scenarios()` in `valuation.py`. The brief dict is also passed to `build_prompt()` in `report_prompts.py` as additional context for the report writing step.

**Context Window Strategy:**

The research loop is not multi-turn from the user's perspective — it is a single function call that produces one structured output. Context management concerns:

- Each web search result is injected as a `web_search_tool_result` block consuming input tokens. At 15 searches, budget 2,000–4,000 input tokens per search result = up to 60K input tokens for search results alone.
- The `RESEARCH_SYSTEM_PROMPT` is fixed at ~400 tokens. Use prompt caching by passing it as a `system` string (the SDK automatically applies cache_control to static system prompts in recent SDK versions — verify in docs if you need explicit cache_control).
- If the loop hits context pressure: reduce `max_uses` to 10, or instruct Claude in the system prompt to be concise in its reasoning text between searches.
- The report writing step (separate `_call_claude_for_report()` call) receives the structured brief as a compact dict — not the full search result conversation — so it is not affected by research loop token volume.

## 4b. AI Systems Best Practices

> Written by `gsd-ai-researcher`. Cross-cutting patterns every developer building AI systems needs — independent of framework choice.

### 4b.1 Structured Outputs with Pydantic

The Anthropic Python SDK does not have a built-in `.with_structured_output()` method like LangChain. For an agentic loop, the correct pattern is: instruct Claude via the system prompt to output a JSON object as its final message, extract the last text block, parse with `json.loads()`, and validate with Pydantic. If validation fails, retry with a correction message.

```python
from pydantic import BaseModel, Field, ValidationError
import json

class ResearchBrief(BaseModel):
    """Validated output of the agentic research loop."""
    company_summary: str = Field(min_length=50)
    sector_summary: str = Field(min_length=50)
    comparable_transactions: str = Field(min_length=20)
    risk_free_rate: float = Field(gt=0, lt=20,
        description="RBNZ 10-yr NZ govt bond yield in percent (e.g. 4.65, not 0.0465)")
    erp: float = Field(gt=0, lt=20,
        description="Damodaran ERP for NZ in percent")
    industry_beta: float = Field(gt=0, lt=10,
        description="Damodaran total beta for the relevant industry")
    industry_category: str = Field(min_length=2,
        description="Damodaran industry category name used for beta lookup")
    inflation_rate: float = Field(gt=-5, lt=30,
        description="Current NZ CPI inflation rate in percent")
    sources: list[str] = Field(min_length=1,
        description="List of source URLs retrieved during research")

    @property
    def wacc_scenarios(self) -> dict:
        """Compute H/M/L WACC from researched inputs. Used by valuation.py."""
        beta_spread = 0.15
        erp_spread = 0.25
        mid = self.risk_free_rate + (self.industry_beta * self.erp)
        return {
            "high": round(mid + beta_spread * self.erp + erp_spread, 2),
            "mid": round(mid, 2),
            "low": round(mid - beta_spread * self.erp - erp_spread, 2),
        }


def parse_and_validate_brief(response: anthropic.types.Message) -> ResearchBrief:
    """
    Extract JSON from the final text block and validate against ResearchBrief.
    Raises ValueError with a diagnostic message on failure — caller decides retry strategy.
    """
    text_blocks = [b for b in response.content if b.type == "text"]
    if not text_blocks:
        raise ValueError("No text block in response — Claude did not produce a final answer")

    raw = text_blocks[-1].text.strip()
    # Strip code fences if Claude added them despite instructions
    if raw.startswith("```"):
        lines = raw.split("\n")
        raw = "\n".join(lines[1:-1] if lines[-1].strip() == "```" else lines[1:])

    try:
        data = json.loads(raw)
    except json.JSONDecodeError as exc:
        raise ValueError(f"Claude response is not valid JSON: {exc}\nRaw: {raw[:500]}") from exc

    try:
        return ResearchBrief(**data)
    except ValidationError as exc:
        # Produce a readable error for the retry message
        errors = "; ".join(f"{e['loc']}: {e['msg']}" for e in exc.errors())
        raise ValueError(f"Research brief validation failed: {errors}") from exc
```

**Retry logic:** If `parse_and_validate_brief()` raises `ValueError`, send a correction turn:

```python
MAX_PARSE_RETRIES = 2

for attempt in range(MAX_PARSE_RETRIES + 1):
    try:
        brief = parse_and_validate_brief(response)
        break
    except ValueError as exc:
        if attempt == MAX_PARSE_RETRIES:
            logger.error("Research brief validation failed after %d retries: %s", attempt, exc)
            raise RuntimeError(f"Could not extract valid research brief: {exc}") from exc
        # Ask Claude to fix its output — pass error message as a user turn
        logger.warning("Parse attempt %d failed (%s) — sending correction turn", attempt + 1, exc)
        messages.append({"role": "assistant", "content": response.content})
        messages.append({
            "role": "user",
            "content": (
                f"Your previous output failed validation with this error: {exc}\n"
                "Please output ONLY the corrected JSON object with no surrounding text."
            )
        })
        response = client.messages.create(
            model=MODEL, max_tokens=8000,
            system=RESEARCH_SYSTEM_PROMPT,
            tools=[WEB_SEARCH_TOOL],
            messages=messages,
        )
```

### 4b.2 Async-First Design

**How async works in this setup:** The Anthropic Python SDK is synchronous by default (it also has an `AsyncAnthropic` client but `run_in_executor` is equally correct and avoids managing a second event loop). The project's established pattern from `ingestion.py` is:

```python
loop = asyncio.get_running_loop()
result = await loop.run_in_executor(None, sync_sdk_call, arg1, arg2)
```

This runs the blocking SDK call on the default `ThreadPoolExecutor` thread pool, freeing the FastAPI event loop for other requests during the 30–90 second research session.

**The one common mistake:** calling `asyncio.run(run_research_loop_sync(...))` from inside a FastAPI route or background task. FastAPI's event loop is already running, so `asyncio.run()` raises `RuntimeError: This event loop is already running`. Always use `loop.run_in_executor()` in an async context.

```python
# WRONG — raises RuntimeError in FastAPI context
result = asyncio.run(run_research_loop_sync(company, location, sector))

# CORRECT — non-blocking, compatible with FastAPI's event loop
loop = asyncio.get_running_loop()
result = await loop.run_in_executor(None, run_research_loop_sync, company, location, sector)
```

**Stream vs. await:** Do not stream the research loop. Streaming is useful for real-time UX (showing tokens as they arrive) but the research loop produces a structured JSON brief that is only useful when complete and validated. Use a blocking `await` on `run_in_executor`. If you want to show research progress to the user, use a background task status field in the DB (e.g., `reports.status = "researching"`) that the frontend polls — not streaming.

**Background task timeout:** The research loop can take 60–120 seconds for 15 searches. Register it as a FastAPI `BackgroundTask` rather than blocking the HTTP response. Return `{"status": "researching", "report_id": id}` immediately, and update `reports.status` to `"done"` or `"failed"` when the loop completes.

### 4b.3 Prompt Engineering Discipline

**System vs. user prompt separation:**
- `RESEARCH_SYSTEM_PROMPT` (system): research methodology, output schema, field definitions, CRITICAL constraints (do not estimate WACC inputs). This is static and should be prompt-cached.
- User message: company name, location, sector — dynamic per request. Never embed the JSON schema in the user message; it belongs in the system prompt so the cache key is stable.

**Instruct Claude on research termination, not just output format.** The most important prompt instruction for an agentic loop is telling Claude when to stop searching. Without an explicit stop condition, Claude may keep searching for increasingly marginal improvements. Add to the system prompt:

```
When you have retrieved: (1) a verified risk-free rate from RBNZ, (2) ERP and beta from Damodaran,
(3) current NZ CPI, and (4) at least 2-3 sentences each on company and sector — stop searching and
output the JSON brief. Do not search for additional confirmation of data you have already found.
```

**Set `max_tokens` explicitly.** Never leave `max_tokens` unbounded in production. For the research loop, `max_tokens=8000` is sufficient for a complete brief plus citations. For the report writing step (separate call), `max_tokens=12000` covers all 12 valuation sections. The Anthropic API will error if `max_tokens` exceeds the model's output limit (claude-sonnet-4-6: 8192 output tokens) — verify the current limit in docs.

**Few-shot examples:** Do not embed few-shot examples for the research loop system prompt — examples are static and waste tokens across the 15-search input token budget. The JSON schema in the system prompt is sufficient for output format. If Claude consistently produces malformed JSON, add one inline example in the system prompt with a `<example>` tag rather than multiple few-shot turns.

### 4b.4 Context Window Management

**Research loop context growth pattern:** Each `messages.create()` call in the loop sends the entire message history, including all `server_tool_use` and `web_search_tool_result` blocks from previous searches within this turn. For claude-sonnet-4-6, the context window is 200K tokens. A 15-search session produces roughly:

- System prompt: ~400 tokens
- User message: ~50 tokens
- Per search: `server_tool_use` (~20 tokens) + `web_search_tool_result` (~2,000–4,000 tokens) × 15 searches = up to 60,000 tokens
- Claude's reasoning text between searches: ~1,000–3,000 tokens
- Final JSON brief + citations: ~800 tokens

Total: ~65,000 tokens — well within the 200K window. No compaction or truncation is needed for a single research session.

**Pause_turn resume:** When `stop_reason == "pause_turn"`, the partial response content is appended to `messages` before the next call. This means the `messages` list grows to include one additional turn. The token overhead is the length of the partial assistant content already generated — typically a few hundred tokens. No action needed beyond the resume pattern in Section 4.

**Report writing context:** The report writing step receives a compact inputs dict (~500 tokens) derived from the research brief — not the full research conversation. Keep the research brief's `company_summary` and `sector_summary` under 1,000 words each to avoid bloating the report writing prompt. Instruct Claude in the research system prompt: "Be concise — each narrative field should be 2-3 paragraphs maximum."

**If context pressure occurs in future** (e.g., `max_uses` is raised to 30+): reduce `max_uses` first, or split the research into two calls — one for WACC inputs (critical, structured) and one for narrative context (company + sector + comps).

### 4b.5 Cost and Latency Budget

**Per-run cost estimate (claude-sonnet-4-6, May 2026 pricing):**

| Cost component | Estimate | Basis |
|----------------|----------|-------|
| Web search requests | $0.15 | 15 searches × $10/1,000 |
| Input tokens (search results + context) | ~$0.09 | ~30,000 input tokens × $3/M |
| Output tokens (reasoning + brief) | ~$0.05 | ~5,000 output tokens × $15/M |
| Report writing call (separate) | ~$0.08 | ~10K input + ~4K output tokens |
| **Total per valuation run** | **~$0.37** | |

At current volume (Bayleys internal tool, estimated 5–20 valuations/month), total AI cost is $2–7/month. No cost optimisation is required at this volume.

**Latency budget:** Research loop: 60–90 seconds (15 searches × ~4–6 seconds per search). Report writing: 20–40 seconds. Total: ~90–120 seconds end-to-end. This is acceptable for a background task. Display `"Researching..."` status in the frontend while the background task runs.

**Caching strategy:**

- Prompt caching: the static `RESEARCH_SYSTEM_PROMPT` is automatically cached by the Anthropic SDK (add explicit `cache_control: {"type": "ephemeral"}` if using the system block list format, as in `ingestion.py`). Cache hit saves ~400 tokens per call — marginal benefit for a loop with 15-search token volume, but implement it for consistency with the codebase pattern.
- Result caching: do not cache research briefs across different valuation requests. WACC inputs change with RBNZ decisions and Damodaran annual updates. Caching stale WACC data into a valuation report is a critical failure mode (see Section 1).
- Sub-task model routing: the research loop uses `claude-sonnet-4-6` for quality — accurate WACC source identification requires strong instruction following. Do not route to Haiku for cost saving; the risk of Haiku hallucinating WACC inputs outweighs the $0.20 saving per run.

## 6. Guardrails

### Online (Real-Time)

These run synchronously inside `run_research_loop_sync()` before the `ResearchBrief` is returned to the caller. Each adds negligible latency (pure Python, no API call).

| Guardrail | Trigger Condition | Code Location | Intervention |
|-----------|-------------------|---------------|--------------|
| **WACC decimal-form detection** | `brief.risk_free_rate < 1.0` or `brief.erp < 1.0` — indicates the agent returned a decimal (0.0465) instead of a percent (4.65), a factor-of-10 error | `run_research_loop_sync()`, immediately after `ResearchBrief(**raw_dict)` | Raise `ValueError("WACC input appears to be in decimal form — risk_free_rate={v}. Expected percent (e.g. 4.65, not 0.0465).")` — triggers Pydantic retry loop; do not pass to DCF |
| **WACC scenario out-of-range** | Computed `brief.wacc_scenarios["mid"] < 8.0` or `> 20.0` after decimal guard passes | `run_research_loop_sync()`, after WACC scenarios are computed | Raise `ValueError("Computed mid-WACC={v}% is outside the valid NZ private SME range of 8–20%. Inputs: rfr={rfr}, beta={beta}, erp={erp}.")` — triggers retry or surfaces as a report generation error; prevents corrupted EV from reaching the report |
| **Required field placeholder detection** | Any of `risk_free_rate`, `erp`, `industry_beta`, `industry_category`, `company_summary`, `sector_summary` contains the strings "N/A", "TBC", "to be confirmed", or is an empty string after stripping | `run_research_loop_sync()`, after Pydantic validation | Raise `ValueError("Research brief contains placeholder value in field '{field}': '{value}'. Research did not complete successfully.")` — do not return a brief with unfilled fields to the DCF |
| **Missing RBNZ/Damodaran source** | `brief.sources` contains no URL matching `rbnz\.govt\.nz` AND no URL matching `damodaran` (case-insensitive) | `run_research_loop_sync()`, after Pydantic validation | Raise `ValueError("Research brief sources do not include an RBNZ or Damodaran URL. WACC inputs cannot be verified as sourced from authoritative data.")` — block report generation; flag in logs as a WACC sourcing failure |
| **FMCA disclaimer presence** | The `disclaimer` section of the generated report does not contain all four required phrases: "indicative", "financial advice", "FMCA" (or "Financial Markets Conduct"), "not relied" | `_call_claude_for_report()` after the report JSON is parsed, before `reports.content` is written to DB | Block DB write; log `ERROR disclaimer_incomplete report_id={id} missing_phrases={list}`; set `reports.status = "disclaimer_error"` so the Bayleys advisor is notified rather than receiving a non-compliant report |

### Offline (Flywheel)

These run as a batch job against sampled completed reports. At current volume (5–20 runs/month), run the full batch weekly rather than on a schedule.

| Metric | What Is Measured | Sampling Strategy | Action on Degradation |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|-----------------------|
| **WACC scenario distribution** | Distribution of `wacc_scenarios.mid` values across all completed reports in the past month; flag if median shifts by more than 1.5 percentage points from the prior month's median | 100% of completed valuation reports (full population at current volume) | If median mid-WACC drifts > 1.5pp, manually inspect the 3 most recent reports; check whether RBNZ published a new OCR decision that explains the shift (legitimate) or whether the agent is retrieving stale data (bug) |
| **Source URL domain diversity** | Count distinct top-level domains in `sources` lists across all reports; flag if `rbnz.govt.nz` or `damodaran.com` appears in fewer than 80% of reports | 100% of completed reports | If RBNZ or Damodaran URL presence drops below 80%, the agent may be finding substitute pages (e.g., a cached aggregator) — review the 3 most recent reports that lacked the canonical URL |
| **Comparable transaction source traceability** | For each report, check whether `comparable_transactions` text contains at least one URL or identifiable deal name that matches a URL in `sources` | Weekly batch across all reports from that week | If more than 20% of reports have comparable transactions with zero traceable source URLs, escalate to the CAANZ valuator for a sample review — potential fabrication pattern |
| **NZ market grounding score** | LLM judge run offline against `sector_summary` for each report: "Does this sector summary include at least one NZ-specific market reference?" — output PASS/FAIL; track weekly PASS rate | 100% of completed reports; judge runs offline in batch | If weekly PASS rate drops below 85%, review prompt for NZ geographic bias instruction; check whether Anthropic's `user_location: NZ` setting is still functioning |
| **Report generation cost per run** | Input tokens + output tokens + web search count per valuation run, logged from `response.usage`; alert if any single run exceeds $1.00 (approximately 3× the expected $0.37) | All runs (logged automatically via Phoenix tracing) | Alert Slack/email if any run exceeds $1.00; investigate whether the agent entered a search loop on a rare sector type and near the `max_uses=15` ceiling |

## 7. Production Monitoring

**Tracing Tool:** Arize Phoenix (open-source, self-hosted on the same machine as the FastAPI backend). No account required. Local UI at `http://localhost:6006`. Traces are persisted to a local SQLite database by Phoenix. At 5–20 runs/month, this is sufficient without a cloud tracing platform.

**Why not cloud tracing at current volume:** Langfuse (self-hosted or cloud) and LangSmith (commercial) both add operational overhead (Docker compose for self-hosted Langfuse, or a platform account for LangSmith) that is not justified at 5–20 runs/month with no LangChain dependency. Phoenix is a single `pip install` with no external dependencies.

**Upgrade path:** If volume exceeds 50 runs/month or the team adds a second developer, migrate to Langfuse self-hosted (Docker compose, Postgres backend) for better multi-user dashboards and team-level alerting.

### Key Metrics to Track (Phoenix custom attributes per span)

| Metric | Attribute Name | Type | What It Signals |
|--------|---------------|------|-----------------|
| Web search count per run | `search_count` | int | Under-research (< 3) or near-ceiling (14–15); both are warning signals |
| Mid-WACC scenario value | `wacc_mid` | float | Distribution drift — compare week-over-week median |
| RBNZ URL present in sources | `sources_has_rbnz` | bool | False = WACC inputs may not be from authoritative source |
| Damodaran URL present in sources | `sources_has_damodaran` | bool | False = beta/ERP inputs may not be from authoritative source |
| Total input tokens | `input_tokens` | int | Cost tracking; spike detection |
| Total output tokens | `output_tokens` | int | Cost tracking |
| Loop iteration count | `loop_iterations` | int | > 3 iterations indicates frequent `pause_turn` — latency signal |
| Research loop wall-clock time (seconds) | `loop_duration_s` | float | > 150s = latency budget exceeded |
| Pydantic validation passed on first attempt | `brief_parse_attempts` | int | > 1 = Claude produced malformed JSON; > 2 = systemic prompt issue |
| Disclaimer compliance check | `disclaimer_ok` | bool | False = FMCA guardrail fired; report blocked |

### Alert Thresholds

Alerts are implemented as log-level `ERROR` entries in the FastAPI logger, with optional Slack webhook (add `SLACK_ALERT_WEBHOOK` to `.env` when team is operational). At current volume, email/Slack is sufficient — no PagerDuty required.

| Condition | Threshold | Log Level | Action |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|--------|
| WACC decimal-form error fired | Any occurrence | ERROR | Log full brief dict for inspection; notify Bayleys advisor that report generation failed |
| WACC out-of-range error fired | Any occurrence | ERROR | Log inputs (`rfr`, `beta`, `erp`); notify advisor; do not deliver report |
| Disclaimer compliance check failed | Any occurrence | ERROR | Block DB write; set `reports.status = "disclaimer_error"`; notify advisor |
| Single run cost exceeds $1.00 | Token + search cost > $1.00 | WARNING | Log run details; manually inspect search trace in Phoenix |
| Loop duration exceeds 150 seconds | `loop_duration_s > 150` | WARNING | Log; no intervention — background task is tolerant of latency; investigate if recurring |
| `brief_parse_attempts > 1` on more than 20% of runs in a week | Rolling weekly ratio | WARNING | Review `RESEARCH_SYSTEM_PROMPT` JSON schema instructions; consider adding a `<example>` tag |
| `sources_has_rbnz = False` on more than 2 consecutive runs | Sequential count | ERROR | Agent may be blocked from rbnz.govt.nz or the page structure changed; check manually |

### Smart Sampling Strategy for Human Review

At 5–20 runs/month, all completed reports are reviewed by the Bayleys advisor before delivery to the SME client. This is the production quality gate. The following signals weight which reports require deeper CAANZ valuator review (beyond the advisor's standard check):

| Signal | Filter | Review Depth |
|--------|--------|--------------|
| `wacc_mid` is outside 10–15% | High or low outlier | CAANZ valuator reviews WACC assumptions table and compares against current RBNZ/Damodaran data |
| `sources_has_rbnz = False` OR `sources_has_damodaran = False` | Missing authoritative source | CAANZ valuator manually verifies WACC inputs against current published data |
| `brief_parse_attempts > 1` | Claude struggled to produce valid JSON | Engineering review of the raw response to determine if the prompt needs adjustment |
| `search_count < 4` | Under-research | Bayleys advisor flags whether company narrative and sector summary appear thin; if so, regenerate |
| `search_count = 15` (hit ceiling) | Max searches used | Bayleys advisor checks whether comparable transactions or WACC data feels incomplete; if so, increase `max_uses` cap |
| `disclaimer_ok = False` | FMCA guardrail fired | Engineering review before any attempt to regenerate; disclaimer failure indicates a prompt regression |
| Sector is agriculture, healthcare, or early-stage startup | Domain edge case | CAANZ valuator reviews Damodaran category selection; these sectors have high category-mismatch risk |
