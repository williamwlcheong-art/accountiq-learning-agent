# Phase 999.1: Public-Facing Commercial Funnel + Advisor Review Model

## Capture

**Date:** 2026-07-01
**Source:** Application backlog planning voice note + BizVal reference screenshots
**Status:** Backlog parking lot. Not sequenced into v1 until reviewed.

## Goal

AccountIQ needs a public-facing product layer around the existing report-generation app: a marketing site, pricing/order journey, attribution, CRM/lead capture, payment experiment, and advisor-review operating model. The working app remains the core, but the commercial experience should feel closer to BizVal: clear fees, trust signals, an order flow, professional credentials, and a credible human reviewer behind the report.

## Strategic Notes

- Todd should be public-facing. He should appear as the valuation/report review lead and the person behind the professional review process.
- Dave should remain the technical/product/report-engine brain behind the system rather than the primary front-facing advisor, because the product may overlap with existing advisory work.
- The product should not simply automate and send reports without review. A Todd-led review queue is both a trust layer and a source of higher-value consulting upsells.
- If demand grows, Todd may need an internal reviewer workflow so additional reviewers can be hired without changing the customer-facing promise.
- The product should make room for consulting upsells after review, especially forecasting, capital raising support, enterprise/advisory work, and bespoke valuation follow-up.

## Reference Inspiration

BizVal screenshots show the useful shape of a public-facing competitor:

- Navigation: Home, Why, Our Service, Our Fees, FAQ, Case Studies, Contact Us, Order Now.
- Trust signal: Chartered Accountants Australia + New Zealand logo in the header.
- Fee-page positioning: fixed-rate fee structure, upfront pricing, no hidden surprises, value for investment.
- Visible fee anchors in the screenshots: Small $2,250 + GST, Medium $3,400 + GST, Large/Start-up $4,500 + GST, Court Standard $5,200 + GST.
- Order journey: order page, downloadable order form alternative, three-step explanation, invoice details, upload recent financial statements.

These should be treated as market reference points, not final AccountIQ pricing or copy.

## Funnel Hypothesis

Likely first public journey:

1. Visitor lands on marketing homepage.
2. Visitor sees report types, pricing/fees, Todd/reviewer credibility, and sample output quality.
3. Visitor clicks Get Started / Order Now.
4. Visitor creates an account or signs in.
5. Visitor uploads financial statements and answers report-specific intake questions.
6. Payment step depends on experiment decision:
   - pay upfront by card,
   - authorize card and capture after review/delivery,
   - upload first and unlock full report after a teaser/preview,
   - invoice/manual review for high-value or edge cases.
7. System generates draft report.
8. Todd/reviewer reviews, approves, requests clarification, or flags an upsell.
9. Customer receives web/PDF delivery and optional consulting CTA.

## Draft Backlog Requirements

- **PUB-01 Marketing site:** Build a public homepage and supporting pages for Why, Services, Fees/Pricing, FAQ, Case Studies, About/Team, Contact, and Order/Get Started.
- **PUB-02 Public credibility:** Present Todd as the public reviewer/advisor; include credentials, CAANZ-style trust signals where legally permitted, profile photos, and a clear "reviewed by a professional" promise.
- **PUB-03 Analytics/attribution:** Add PostHog or equivalent for page views, click tracking, UTM/source attribution, ad campaign attribution, funnel steps, upload starts, upload completes, payment starts, payment completes, and abandonment points.
- **PUB-04 CRM/lead capture:** Choose a lightweight/free CRM or lead store for contact forms, abandoned-start leads, downloadable sample/report requests, and consulting follow-up.
- **PUB-05 Pricing/order model:** Define public fee model by report type and/or company size, using BizVal as a reference anchor but deciding AccountIQ-specific packages.
- **PUB-06 Payment experiment:** Decide whether to require credit card upfront, delayed capture, invoice-after-review, or teaser/preview-first unlock. This directly affects Stripe scope and copy.
- **PUB-07 Human review operations:** Add admin-side queue for Todd/reviewers to review generated drafts, approve delivery, request more info, assign reviewers, leave internal notes, and mark consulting opportunities.
- **PUB-08 Upsell workflow:** Add post-review and post-delivery prompts for forecasting support, capital raising, valuation discussion, and enterprise/advisory work.
- **PUB-09 Infrastructure decision:** Reconcile the Next.js refactor plan with deployment direction. Likely public site/app frontend in Next.js, FastAPI retained for ingestion/report backend, Railway + Postgres considered for managed deployment.
- **PUB-10 Compliance/commercial guardrails:** Terms, privacy policy, refund policy, indicative-only disclaimers, professional-review wording, permission to use logos/credentials, and clear boundary between AI draft and advisor review.

## Open Decisions

- Should the brand stay AccountIQ, move toward BizVal-style positioning, or create a new public brand around Todd?
- Is the first launch one report type only (valuation advisory) or all five report types?
- Is pricing flat, tiered by turnover/report type, or hybrid?
- Is the first payment flow card upfront, authorization hold, invoice, or preview/unlock?
- What exactly does Todd review before a report is released: all reports, valuation only, exceptions only, or paid review add-on?
- What are the legal constraints around CAANZ logo usage, advisor credentials, and "reviewed by" wording?
- Which CRM is acceptable for launch: PostHog-only event capture, a free CRM, or a custom leads table?
- Should public marketing pages ship in the Next.js refactor before the old vanilla app is retired?
