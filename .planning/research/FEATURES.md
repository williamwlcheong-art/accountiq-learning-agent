# Features Research

## Report Type Specifications

### 1. Valuation Report
**Standard sections:**
1. Executive Summary (concluded value range, methodology used)
2. Business Overview (description, products/services, market position)
3. Financial Performance (3-5 years: revenue, EBITDA, margins, growth rates)
4. EBITDA Normalisation (add-backs: owner salary excess, one-off costs, related-party rents)
5. Valuation Methodology (why DCF and/or multiples were used)
6. Market Comparable Multiples (industry EV/EBITDA range, source, selected multiple)
7. DCF Analysis (revenue forecast, discount rate rationale, terminal value)
8. Concluded Value Range (low/mid/high)
9. Key Value Drivers (what moves the needle)
10. Risks & Mitigants

**Key metrics required:** Revenue CAGR (3yr), Normalised EBITDA, EBITDA margin, Net debt, EV/EBITDA multiple applied, implied enterprise value, implied equity value

**Data inputs beyond financials:** Industry/sector, ownership structure, capex requirements, key customer concentration, owner's current salary vs market rate, one-off/non-recurring expenses

**Typical length:** 18-25 pages

**First-draft quality bar:** Correct industry multiple applied, normalised EBITDA clearly derived, value range with low/high spread, DCF assumptions stated explicitly (not hidden)

### 2. Bank Credit Paper
**Standard sections:**
1. Transaction Summary (borrower, loan amount, purpose, proposed structure)
2. Borrower Overview (business description, years operating, ownership)
3. Financial Analysis — Income Statement (3yr trend, margins)
4. Financial Analysis — Balance Sheet (assets, liabilities, net worth)
5. Financial Analysis — Cash Flow (operating cash flow, free cash flow)
6. Debt Serviceability (DSCR, interest coverage, debt/EBITDA)
7. Sensitivity Analysis (DSCR at -10%, -20% revenue)
8. Security & Collateral
9. Risk Assessment (key risks and mitigants)
10. Recommendation

**Key metrics required:** DSCR (>1.25x typical requirement), interest coverage ratio, debt/EBITDA (<3.0x typical), current ratio, days receivable/payable, net debt/equity

**Data inputs beyond financials:** Loan amount requested, loan purpose, proposed term, proposed security/collateral offered

**Typical length:** 10-15 pages

**First-draft quality bar:** DSCR calculated correctly, sensitivity table present, risk section balanced (not just positive), recommendation is conditional not binary

### 3. Financial Forecast
**Standard sections:**
1. Executive Summary (key forecast outputs, growth thesis)
2. Historical Performance (3yr actuals as baseline)
3. Key Assumptions (revenue drivers, cost structure, growth rates — must be explicit)
4. Revenue Forecast (by segment if applicable, 3-5 years)
5. Cost Forecast (COGS, opex, headcount-linked costs)
6. EBITDA & Margin Forecast
7. Cash Flow Forecast (operating, investing, financing)
8. Balance Sheet Forecast
9. Scenario Analysis (Base / Bull / Bear — at minimum revenue +/-20%)
10. Sensitivity Table

**Key metrics required:** Revenue growth rate (YoY), EBITDA margin, free cash flow, net debt evolution

**Data inputs beyond financials:** Key growth assumptions (new contracts, new products, market expansion), major planned capex, hiring plans

**Typical length:** 12-20 pages

**First-draft quality bar:** Assumptions page is explicit and defensible, scenarios are meaningfully different (not just ±5%), balance sheet ties to cash flow statement

---

### 4. Capital Raising Document
**Standard sections:**
1. Executive Summary / Investment Highlights (1-page hook)
2. Investment Thesis (why invest now)
3. Business Overview (what the business does, competitive position)
4. Market Opportunity (TAM/SAM — can be qualitative for SMEs)
5. Business Model & Revenue Streams
6. Financial Performance (3yr actuals, key metrics)
7. Financial Projections (3yr forward)
8. Use of Funds (specific allocation of the raise)
9. Management Team (founder + key staff bios)
10. Investment Terms (equity %, valuation, minimum ticket — may be TBD)

**Key metrics required:** Revenue, EBITDA, growth rates, implied valuation, equity offered, funding ask amount

**Data inputs beyond financials:** Funding amount, equity offered, team bios, use of funds breakdown, market context

**Typical length:** 12-18 pages

**First-draft quality bar:** Investment thesis is distinct (not generic), use of funds is specific (not "working capital"), team section is substantive

### 5. Information Memorandum (IM)
**Standard sections:**
1. Transaction Overview (process, confidentiality, indicative timeline)
2. Executive Summary (business snapshot, headline financials, asking price if disclosed)
3. Business Overview (history, products/services, geographic footprint)
4. Market & Competitive Landscape
5. Operations (facilities, supply chain, technology, IP)
6. Management & Employees (org structure, key person dependencies)
7. Customers & Sales (customer concentration, contracts, pipeline)
8. Financial Performance (5yr P&L, balance sheet, cash flow)
9. Financial Projections
10. Transaction Structure (what's being sold: assets vs shares, conditions)
11. Appendices (financial statements, org chart, lease summaries)

**Key metrics required:** Revenue, EBITDA, SDE (Seller's Discretionary Earnings — critical for SME IMs), customer concentration (top 5 customers as % of revenue), employee count, key assets

**Data inputs beyond financials:** Reason for sale, asking price or range, management retention intentions, employee count, lease terms, IP/trademarks, key customer/supplier relationships

**Typical length:** 30-45 pages (the longest report type)

**First-draft quality bar:** Confidentiality notice present, no financial projections without stated assumptions, customer concentration disclosed, key-person risk addressed honestly

## Table Stakes (users expect these)

- **Accurate financial data** — extraction errors in a report users pay for will generate immediate refund demands and destroy trust
- **Professional visual formatting** — looks like a document from a Big 4 firm, not a Word doc
- **Industry context** — multiples, benchmarks, sector commentary; without this the report is generic
- **Narrative text throughout** — numbers alone are not a report; context and interpretation are required
- **PDF download** — must be shareable with banks, advisors, investors without them needing app access
- **Report covers 3+ years of financials** — single-year analysis is not credible for valuation or lending
- **Normalised/adjusted EBITDA** — the signature value-add for SME reports; without it the report misses the point
- **Disclaimer / indicative-only language** — legal protection and user expectation setting

## Differentiators (competitive advantage)

- **EBITDA add-back wizard** — guided flow for owner to identify and quantify adjustments; most competitors skip this
- **Scenario analysis** — bull/base/bear built into every report that has projections
- **Tailored industry multiples** — sourced per sector, not a generic "4-6x EBITDA"
- **Web viewer before PDF** — user can review and confirm before downloading; reduces "wrong report" complaints
- **Extraction confidence indicators** — flag which line items were uncertain so user can verify before generating

## Anti-Features (deliberately skip in v1)

- **Live market data feeds** (Bloomberg, CapIQ) — cost, complexity, and licensing; use curated static multiple ranges instead
- **CPA/auditor sign-off workflow** — not a regulated service; adding approval flows adds liability not value
- **Multi-currency** — all reports in the currency of the uploaded financials; FX conversion deferred
- **Comparable transactions database** — sophisticated; static industry multiple ranges are sufficient for first-draft quality
- **White-label / advisor mode** — target is business owners, not resellers; defer to v2
- **API access** — web UI only for v1
- **Report editing in-app** — deliver first-draft PDF; user edits in Word; don't try to be a document editor
